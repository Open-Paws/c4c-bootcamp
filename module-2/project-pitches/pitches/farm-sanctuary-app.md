# KindQuest App/ When They Speak App

**Pitched by:** Leora Kahn
**Category:** Animal Advocacy / Education

## The Problem
Existing apps that touch on sanctuary or shelter animals often lean into a “cutesy,” bubblegum style, reducing animals to collectibles or caretaking tasks rather than helping children connect with them as sentient beings.

## The Tool
An AI-driven app that transforms real sanctuary animal stories into interactive, child-friendly conversations and consent-based interactions. Children can ask questions and engage with animals, receiving grounded, story-based responses that reflect each animal’s personality, preferences, and boundaries. Includes a content system for curating and expanding verified animal narratives.

## Who It Helps
This tool supports farm sanctuaries and animal advocacy organisations by turning real animal stories into interactive experiences, while giving children and families a pathway to form connections and support specific animals through virtual adoption. It also includes a parent-focused section, “Come Visit a Real Sanctuary,” encouraging real-world visits.

## Tech I Think It Needs
- AI/NLP model for generating story-based responses
 - Structured content database for animal profiles and narratives
- Mobile app (Android for now, maybe Flutter later) with child-friendly UI
- Simple state logic for moods, preferences, and consent-based interactions
- Potential backend for future content management and sanctuary integration (adding/editing animal stories)
- Optional: integration with sanctuary websites or donation/adoption platforms

## Why I Care About This
This is a great opportunity to teach kids a level of empathy that is beyond caring for a household pet or feeding a goat in a petting zoo. Through gameplay, they can learn that all animals have inner lives, preferences, and a backstory. It's a gentle, non-preachy way to help kids become aware that the food on their plate comes from individuals with feelings, and to let that understanding grow naturally into more compassionate choices.
